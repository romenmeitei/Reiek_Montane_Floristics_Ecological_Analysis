# Reiek Montane Floristics Ecological Analysis

## Overview

This repository contains the complete reproducible workflow, datasets, and ecological analyses associated with the manuscript:

> **“Floristic Composition and Vegetation Observations from Reiek Montane Forests, Mizoram, India”**

The study documents vascular plant diversity, habitat heterogeneity, epiphytic vegetation, riparian flora, and ethnobotanical significance of the Reiek montane forests located within the Indo-Burma Biodiversity Hotspot of Northeast India.

The repository includes:
- Processed floristic datasets
- Ecological summary tables
- Quantitative visualizations
- Python-based analysis workflows
- Publication-ready figures generated using Google Colaboratory

---

# Study Area

The study was conducted in the Reiek montane landscape, Mizoram, India, during **14–16 April 2025**.

### Surveyed habitats included:
- Evergreen montane forests
- Secondary forest patches
- Riparian ecosystems
- Cliff-associated vegetation
- Shaded ravines
- Village-associated vegetation

### Elevation
- Approximately **1300–1500 m above sea level**

### Biogeographic Region
- **Indo-Burma Biodiversity Hotspot**

---

# Objectives

The study aimed to:

- Document floristic diversity of the Reiek montane forests
- Assess habitat-wise vegetation composition
- Evaluate ecological grouping and habit diversity
- Identify indicator taxa of montane evergreen ecosystems
- Record epiphytic and moisture-sensitive flora
- Document ethnobotanical and economically important plant species
- Generate baseline ecological datasets for future biodiversity studies
